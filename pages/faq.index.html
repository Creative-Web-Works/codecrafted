The website design process can be broken down into four main milestones:

Defining Site Goals: In this initial stage, your appointed website designer will work with you to define the goals and objectives for your website. This involves identifying the target audience and determining the website's purpose and functionality.

Preliminary Design Phase: During this stage, your website designer will create a preliminary design of the website, based on the goals and objectives established in the previous stage. This may include sketches, wireframes, or mockups of the website's layout, as well as the overall look and feel of the site.

Site Structure Phase: Once the preliminary design has been approved, the designer will move on to creating the site structure. This involves developing a sitemap and determining the navigation structure of the site. The designer will also create a content outline and identify any necessary features or functionality.

Design Phase: In the final stage of the design process, the website designer will create the actual design of the site, incorporating all of the elements developed in the previous stages. This includes the visual design, as well as any interactive features, animations, or other elements that will enhance the user experience.

By following these four milestones, your website designer can create a website that meets your goals and objectives, while also providing an exceptional user experience.

Customers can make alterations to their website during two review phases: the Preliminary Design Phase and the Design Phase.

During the Preliminary Design Phase, customers can review and provide feedback on the initial design concepts presented by our team. Based on this feedback, we'll make any necessary changes and move on to the Site Structure Phase.

During the Design Phase, customers can review the final design and make any further alterations before giving their approval.

Once the final review is complete, your site will be deployed and ready for launch!.
